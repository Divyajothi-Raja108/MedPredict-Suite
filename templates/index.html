HEART DISEASE

Heart disease refers to various conditions affecting the heart, including coronary artery disease, arrhythmias, and heart failure. It's a leading cause of death worldwide, often resulting from high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, obesity, and diabetes. Symptoms include chest pain, shortness of breath, and fatigue. Prevention involves a healthy diet, regular exercise, avoiding smoking, and managing stress. Treatments can include medications, lifestyle changes, and surgeries. Early detection and management are key to improving outcomes.

DIABETES

Diabetes is a chronic condition affecting how the body processes glucose (sugar). Type 1 diabetes results from the immune system attacking insulin-producing cells, requiring insulin therapy. Type 2 diabetes involves insulin resistance, often linked to lifestyle factors like obesity and lack of exercise. Both types lead to high blood sugar levels, causing symptoms like excessive thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, and blurred vision. Management involves blood sugar monitoring, healthy eating, physical activity, and sometimes medication to prevent complications such as heart disease and nerve damage.

LIVER DISEASE

Liver cirrhosis is advanced scarring of the liver caused by chronic liver diseases like hepatitis and alcohol abuse. It impairs liver function, leading to symptoms such as fatigue, jaundice, and fluid retention. Complications include bleeding, infections, and liver cancer. Management involves treating underlying causes, lifestyle changes, and sometimes liver transplantation to improve prognosis and quality of life. Early detection and intervention are crucial for managing this progressive condition effectively.
